Dad's Pork

Description

Delicious, tender pork chops every time! This recipe is a go to when I'm short on time and want to make a dinner that everyone in the family will love.

Ingredients

  • Pork chops, approximately 1" thick
  • Dijon Mustard, 1 cup
  • Salt, to taste
  • Pepper, to taste
  • Thyme, to taste
  • Rosemary, to taste
  • Garlic, 2-3 cloves, minced
  • Coconut or olive, or avocado oil, 1-2 tablespoons

Steps

  1. Season the pork with dry rub.

    2. Mix the salt, pepper, rosemary, and thyme together. Rub both sides of each pork chops.

  2. Add wet rub

    3. Mix minced garlic with Dijon mustard in a bowl. Use a basting brush to coat each side of the pork chops.

  3. Sear pork chops.

    4. Heat a skillet at high heat. Add your chosed oil, and then sear the pork chops for about a minute on each side.

  4. Bake the pork

    5. Heat oven to 425°F. Move seared porkchops into a baking dish. Make sure you tranfer all the drippings and from the skillet to the baking dish for flavor.

    Put in the oven and bake for 6-7 minutes, or until the pork reaches an internal temperature of 135°F for medium-rare. Tent the pork with aluminum foil and let rest for 5-10 minutes.

  5. Serve

    6. Serve with your favorite sides. I like serving it with rice with cooked greens or salad.
